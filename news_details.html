the developer behind the regenerative tourism destinations The Red Sea

08/05/2024


Last year RSG opened the Red Sea Mangrove Nursery and has transplanted one million seedlings across its destination areas to date. This year it expects to grow a further two million.

This latest initiative is a part of a larger-scale program called ‘From People to Planet’, where members of the local community, RSG partners and visitors to The Red Sea destination can take part in future planting initiatives.
"Through meticulous site selection, we have achieved a remarkable survival rate of 99% among the transplanted mangrove across our destination areas. But beyond the brains needed to achieve this feat, it takes incredible people power. We extend our heartfelt thanks to the partners and local community members who have volunteered to help in our planting efforts to date. Our hope is that through our ‘From People to Planet’ program others will support our environmental regeneration efforts," Mr. Albasseet added.

Last year, The Red Sea welcomed its first guests, with three of its hotels now open. RSI has been receiving a regular schedule of domestic flights since September 2023 and international flights began in April 2024, with a twice-weekly route between The Red Sea and Dubai International.
Upon full completion in 2030, the destination will comprise 50 resorts, offering up to 8,000 hotel rooms and more than 1,000 residential properties across 22 islands and six inland sites. The destination will also include luxury marinas, golf courses, entertainment, F&B, and leisure facilities.
